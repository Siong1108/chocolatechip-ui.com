Advanced Tableviews

In the earlier tutorial on basic tableviews we saw different way to use celltitles with tablecell indicators, icons and images. There are other useful elements that you can use to present data in a number of ways in a tablecell. These are:

  • Cellsubtitle
  • Stacked Tableviews
  • Stacked Tableviews with Images
  • Centered Tableviews
  • Celldetail
  • Stacked Celldetail
  • Stacked Tableview with Celldetail and Image
  • Celldatetime
  • Cellcounter

 

Cellsubtitle:

Although celltitles provide a nice way to present the user with some information in a tablecell, sometimes you might want to supply some secondary information as well. You can do this using a cellsubtitle tag. By default a cellsubtitle tag is styled in a small font than the celltitle, has a bluish color and gets positioned on the right side of the tablecell. ChocolateChip-UI will truncate both of these with an ellipsis when there is insufficient room for them to both fit in the available tablecell. You can override the positioning and color of the cellsubtitle by using the stacked tableview layout below.

Example:

<tableview>
    <tablecell>
        <celltitle>People</celltitle>
        <cellsubtitle>Your Friends</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
    <tablecell>
        <celltitle>Places</celltitle>
        <cellsubtitle>Interesting Destinations</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
    <tablecell>
        <celltitle>Animals are a lot of fun</celltitle>
        <cellsubtitle>Critters from Around the Globe</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
    <tablecell>
        <celltitle>Food</celltitle>
        <cellsubtitle>Recipes</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
    <tablecell>
        <celltitle>Food</celltitle>
        <cellsubtitle>Restaurants</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
</tableview>

Stacked Tableviews:

If you want to change the location of the cellsubtite from the right to sit underneath the celltitle you can use the ui-tablcell-order attribute to set the tableview to display its contents in stacked format. Just put *ui-tablecell-order="stacked" on the tableview, as in the example below.

Example:

<tableview ui-tablecell-order="stacked">
    <tablecell  ui-implements="disclosure">
        <celltitle>People</celltitle>
        <cellsubtitle>Your Friends</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
    <tablecell  ui-implements="disclosure">
        <celltitle>Places</celltitle>
        <cellsubtitle>Interesting Destinations</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
    <tablecell  ui-implements="disclosure">
        <celltitle>Funny Animals</celltitle>
        <cellsubtitle>Critters from Around the Globe</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
    <tablecell  ui-implements="disclosure">
        <celltitle>Food</celltitle>
        <cellsubtitle>Recipes</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
    <tablecell ui-implements="disclosure">
        <celltitle>Good Eats</celltitle>
        <cellsubtitle>Restaurants</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>

Stacked Tableviews with Images:

Often you may need to display an image in a table cell along with at celltitle and cellsubtitle. This is easy to do. Give the tableview ui-tablecell-order="stacked" and put *ui-usage="image" on the tablecells. The image should be 55 by 55 pixels and should also be the first item in the tablecell, followed by the celltitle and cellsubtitle. Check out the example below.

Example:

<tableview ui-tablecell-order="stacked">
    <tablecell  ui-usage="image" ui-implements="detail-disclosure">
        <img src="images/alternative.png">
        <celltitle>People</celltitle>
        <cellsubtitle>Your Friends</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
    <tablecell ui-usage="image" ui-implements="detail-disclosure">
        <img src="images/blues.png">
        <celltitle>Places</celltitle>
        <cellsubtitle>Interesting Destinations</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
    <tablecell  ui-usage="image" ui-implements="detail-disclosure">
        <img src="images/classical.png">
        <celltitle>Funny Animals</celltitle>
        <cellsubtitle>Critters from Around the Globe</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
    <tablecell  ui-usage="image" ui-implements="detail-disclosure">
        <img src="images/itunes.png">
        <celltitle>Food</celltitle>
        <cellsubtitle>Recipes</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
    <tablecell  ui-usage="image" ui-implements="detail-disclosure">
        <img src="images/killers.png">
        <celltitle>Good Eats</celltitle>
        <cellsubtitle>Restaurants</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
</tableview>

Centered Tableviews:

Sometimes you may want to deemphasis the celltitles and draw the user's attention to the cellsubtitels. You can do that by using ChocolateChip-UI's centered tablecell layout. This is accomplished by putting ui-tablecell-order="centered" on the tableview. When using this type of layout, you only be using celltitles and cellsubtitles, no images or icons. Example:

<tableview ui-tablecell-order="centered">
    <tablecell>
        <celltitle>People</celltitle>
        <cellsubtitle>Your Friends</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
    <tablecell>
        <celltitle>Places</celltitle>
        <cellsubtitle>Interesting Destinations</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
    <tablecell>
        <celltitle>Funny Animals</celltitle>
        <cellsubtitle>Critters from Around the Globe</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
    <tablecell>
        <celltitle>Food</celltitle>
        <cellsubtitle>Recipes</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
    <tablecell>
        <celltitle>Good Eats</celltitle>
        <cellsubtitle>Restaurants</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
</tableview>

Celldetail

Sometimes you need to give the user more detailed information about and item than can be display by a celltitle and cellsubtitle. In those cases you can use celldetails. These always come after the celltitle and cellsubtitle. If you put celldetails into a plain tableview with celltitles and cellsubtitles, the cellsubtitle will be placed on the right and the celldetail will sit below it and the celltitle. If you want the tablecell items to be stacked, celltitle over cellsubtitle over celldetail, you'll need to use the stacked celldetail layout. The example below shows the basic unstacked tableview with celltitle, cellsubtitle and celldetail. Note that I have two celldetails in the first tablecell.

If you need to display information regarding date or time you can do so using the celldatetime tag. For this use a tableview with ui-tablecell-order="stacked" and place the celldatetime tag right after the celltitle tag and before the cellsubtitle tag. ChocolateChip-UI will automatically style this tag as a bluish rounded badge positioned on the right of the tablecell. See the example below.

Celldatetime:

Example:

<tableview ui-tablecell-order="stacked">
    <tablecell ui-implements="disclosure">
        <celltitle>People</celltitle>
        <celldatetime>Monday</celldatetime>
        <cellsubtitle>Your Friends</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
    <tablecell ui-implements="disclosure">
        <celltitle>Places</celltitle>
        <celldatetime>3/14/10</celldatetime>
        <cellsubtitle>Interesting Destinations</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
    <tablecell ui-implements="disclosure">
        <celltitle>Funny Animals</celltitle>
        <celldatetime>12:30 PM</celldatetime>
        <cellsubtitle>Critters from Around the Globe</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
    <tablecell ui-implements="disclosure">
        <celltitle>Food</celltitle>
        <celldatetime>Noon</celldatetime>
        <cellsubtitle>Recipes</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
    <tablecell ui-implements="disclosure">
        <celltitle>Good Eats</celltitle>
        <celldatetime>Next Week</celldatetime>
        <cellsubtitle>Restaurants</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
</tableview>

On occasion your tableview items may have numerical information that you would like to show the user so that they can make decisions based on those numerical values. You can present this numeral information using cellcounters. Put ui-tablecell-order="stacked" on a tableview, then put the cellcounters after the celltitles and before the cellsubtitles. The example below shows a stacked tableview with images, celltitles, cellcounters and cellsuttitles.

Cellcounter:

Example:

<tableview ui-tablecell-order="stacked">
    <tablecell  ui-usage="icon" ui-implements="disclosure">
        <img src="images/ipod.png">
        <celltitle>People</celltitle>
        <cellcounter>2</cellcounter>
        <cellsubtitle>Your Friends</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
    <tablecell  ui-usage="icon" ui-implements="disclosure">
        <img src="images/phone.png">
        <celltitle>Places</celltitle>
        <cellcounter>14</cellcounter>
        <cellsubtitle>Interesting Destinations</cellsubtitle>
    </tablecell>
    <tablecell  ui-usage="icon" ui-implements="disclosure">

        <celltitle>Funny Animals</celltitle>
        <cellcounter>83</cellcounter>
        <cellsubtitle>Critters from Around the Globe</cellsubtitle>
